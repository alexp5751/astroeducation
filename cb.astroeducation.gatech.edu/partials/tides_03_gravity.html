Equation of Gravity

Isaac Newton found a single equation which demonstrates the relationships between distance and mass when computing gravity. The force of gravity between any two objects is equal to the product of the masses of both objects, divided by the square of the distance, all multiplied by a constant.

Mathematics of Mass

Assuming all other variables remain constant, the greater the product of the masses of the objects, the greater the gravitational force. The converse holds true as well. The smaller the product of the masses, the weaker the gravitational force.

Defining Distance

As the distance between the two masses decreases, the greater the force of gravity will be for those two objects. The converse holds true: as the distance between two masses increases, the smaller the force of gravity will be for those objects.
